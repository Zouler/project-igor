# World structure and long-term progression — Proyecto I.G.O.R.

How the **planet / community** and **player progression** are intended to grow over the life of the product.

---

## World presentation

- **Zoomed-out planet / community map** — diorama readability; see progress at a glance.  
- **Machines are placed** onto **empty terrain plots** (or designated pads) once deployment exists.  
- **Terrain states** can include (over time): dirty, rocky, uneven, blocked, **unlit**, empty, or **ready for construction**.  
- Machines work **autonomously or semi-autonomously** once deployed — watchable, toy-like performances rather than stressful micromanagement (tone TBD per machine).

---

## Terrain and task examples (machine roles)

Illustrative **jobs** machines might perform as the roster expands:

| Role | Example effect |
|------|----------------|
| **Digger** | Clears rocks, digs holes, prepares terrain |
| **Sweeper** | Cleans terrain / debris |
| **Roller** | Levels terrain, creates roads |
| **Builder** | Constructs houses, bridges, facilities |
| **Carrier** | Moves blocks / materials |
| **Scavenger** | Collects resources |
| **Lightbot** | Eventually wakes **light towers** (ties to Mission 3 hook) |

Not all exist in the MVP; this is the **expansion vocabulary**.

---

## Civilization progression

- **Camp starts small** — a few structures and clear needs.  
- **Machines gather materials** — feeding the construction loop.  
- The child **builds structures** using **blocks** (Construction Area).  
- **Structures unlock** more **parts**, **facilities**, and **spaceship repair components** over time.

Progress should feel like **the planet waking up**, not a grim survival grind.

---

## Ship reconstruction (endgame direction)

Later **buildings / facilities** produce or unlock **ship parts**, for example:

- Panels  
- Larger engines  
- Doors, windows, seats  
- Navigation parts  
- Launch platform components  

**Larger ship engines** may also house **“the spark”** — the living energy theme consistent with Motorlings.

- The ship **eventually becomes alive / aware enough** to **land safely**.  
- **Endgame:** the child can **choose to return home**.  
- **Post-game:** the child can **return to I.G.O.R.’s planet whenever they want** — the bond persists.

---

## Endgame tone

**Not sad.** Leaving is a **choice** and a **triumph**, not a loss. The child goes home but keeps a **living connection** to I.G.O.R. and the planet (emotional through-line, not necessarily a live-service requirement in v1).

---

## MVP vs long-term

**MVP:** Mission-based workshop + scripted test zones + **Community** percentage and copy as a **slice** of “world improves.”  

**Long-term:** Full map interaction, construction yard, facilities, scavenger loops, and ship parts as described above.
