# Proyecto I.G.O.R. — Visual Identity Bible v1

This document captures the **current visual direction** aligned with existing concept imagery. It is the **evolving visual bible** for the project. Implementation in-engine may still use placeholders until art passes are approved.

---

## Core visual pillars

- **Toy-like mechanical world** — readable, playful, not industrial horror.
- **Warm child-friendly steampunk-lite** — brass warmth, not cold sci-fi.
- **Diorama / living miniature world** — the planet feels like a collectible scene you could hold.
- **Handmade inventor workshop feeling** — painted wood, personal touches, tools and scraps.
- **Worn but lovable machinery** — patina and charm, not decay as dread.
- **Clear readable shapes for ages 5+** — silhouettes and primary forms read at a glance.
- **Warm, emotional, hopeful tone** — the world is waking up, not ending.

---

## I.G.O.R. character direction

- **Friendly old yellow robot** — approachable elder energy, not intimidating.
- **Yellow ochre / mustard painted metal** — signature warmth; avoid cold gray as dominant.
- **Slight oxidation and scratches** — lived-in, decorative, never grotesque.
- **Big expressive eyes** — emotion and connection for young players.
- **Rounded rectangular head** — soft geometry, toy-like.
- **Slim springy limbs** — light, bouncy, slightly awkward motion language.
- **Large stable feet** — grounded, safe, huggable proportions.
- **Small antenna** — signal of curiosity; optional playful wobble.
- **Visible “I.G.O.R.” chest tag / nameplate** — identity and merchandising clarity.
- **Personality:** curious, lovable, slightly awkward, **never scary**.

---

## World direction

- **Zoomed-out diorama / community map** — overview of progress and stories.
- **Small mechanical planet slowly waking up** — narrative of restoration, not conquest.
- **Green soft terrain + mechanical ruins + warm workshop pieces** — nature and machine in balance.
- **Empty plots** where machines can be deployed — clear affordances for future placement.
- **Roads, bridges, houses, workshops, facilities** — civilization growing in toy-readable chunks.
- **Colorful toy-like buildings and clear mission markers** — navigation without a dense HUD.

---

## UI direction

- **Large touch-friendly buttons** — tablet-first.
- **Child-readable text** — generous sizes; avoid dense paragraphs in play.
- **Rounded panels** — soft containers, not sharp terminal chrome.
- **Workshop signs / metal plates / painted wood** — diegetic flavor where it helps.
- **Strong color coding** — mission state, actions, and feedback distinguishable quickly.
- **Avoid tiny text and overloaded screens** — one clear focus per moment when possible.

---

## Materials (target language)

- Warm worn yellow metal  
- Painted wood  
- Soft rubber wheels  
- Brass / bronze accents  
- Oxidized but **friendly** metal  
- Simple glowing spark materials (Motorlings / energy)  
- Clean toy-like ground and path materials  

---

## Do

- Make objects **readable from a distance**.
- Use **warm** colors and lighting.
- Keep shapes **soft and rounded** where appropriate for the age band.
- Make I.G.O.R. **emotionally expressive**.
- Keep oxidation **decorative and charming**, not gritty realism.

## Do not

- Make the world **dark, horror-like, industrial, cold sci-fi, or post-apocalyptic**.
- Make I.G.O.R. **too realistic, scary, or overcomplicated**.
- Use **tiny UI** or illegible type on target devices.
- **Sacrifice child readability** for surface detail.

---

## Governance note

The visual identity in this bible should still be **debated and approved** by the core team before **major aesthetic implementation** or large asset-generation prompts are executed. Treat **v1** as the shared starting point, not an immutable contract.
